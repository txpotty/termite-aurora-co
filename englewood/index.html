Comprehensive Termite Control Services: What We Offer

At Aurora Termite Control, our approach to termite control in Englewood is thorough, effective, and tailored to your specific needs. Detailed Termite Inspection

The first and most crucial step in effective termite control is a comprehensive inspection. Our experienced technicians will conduct a meticulous examination of your property, both interior and exterior, to identify any signs of termite activity, locate colonies, and assess the extent of the damage. This involves:

Visual Inspection

Our technicians will visually inspect vulnerable areas such as basements, crawl spaces, attics, and areas around foundations and wooden structures. We look for common indicators of termites, including mud tubes, damaged wood, discarded wings, and pinpoint holes in drywall or wood surfaces. Special attention is paid to areas with high moisture levels, as termites are attracted to damp environments. We also examine exterior elements like wood piles, fences, and tree stumps that could be potential entry points or nesting sites.

Moisture Meters

Using specialized moisture meters, we can detect elevated moisture levels within walls, floors, and other structures, even if there are no visible signs of damage. Subterranean termites require moisture to survive, and high moisture areas often indicate potential termite activity or conditions conducive to infestation. This non-invasive tool helps us identify hidden areas that might be attractive to termites.

Sounding Devices

In some cases, our technicians may use sounding devices to tap on wooden surfaces and listen for hollow sounds, which can indicate termite damage within the wood. This technique helps us pinpoint areas of structural weakness caused by termites that might not be immediately visible. Subtle differences in sound can reveal hidden tunnels and galleries created by termites as they consume wood.

The detailed inspection allows us to accurately diagnose the type of termites present, understand the scope of the infestation, and develop a targeted treatment plan that is most effective for your specific situation. We provide you with a detailed report of our findings and recommendations.

Targeted Treatment Solutions

Based on the findings of our inspection, we will recommend and implement the most appropriate treatment solution for your termite infestation. We utilize a variety of proven methods, depending on the type of termites, the severity of the infestation, and the construction of your property. Our goal is to eliminate the active colony while minimizing impact on your home and the environment where possible.

Liquid Termiticide Barriers

For subterranean termites, creating a protective liquid barrier around the foundation of your home is a highly effective treatment. We apply a specialized termiticide to the soil around the perimeter of your property, creating a zone that termites cannot cross without being exposed to the treatment. This barrier eliminates termites that attempt to enter or exit your home and can provide long-lasting protection. We carefully inject the termiticide into the soil at specific intervals to ensure complete and consistent coverage.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems are another effective approach, particularly for active termite colonies. These systems involve strategically placing bait stations around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting insect growth regulator that termites consume and carry back to their colony, effectively spreading the treatment. This method can eliminate the entire colony over time and is often preferred in situations where liquid treatments may not be feasible or desirable. Monitoring of the bait stations is crucial to ensure the system is working effectively.

Direct Application to Infested Areas

In cases of localized termite activity, we may apply targeted treatments directly to infested areas within your home. This could involve injecting termiticide into wall voids, damaged wood, or other areas where termites are present. This method is often used in conjunction with other treatments to address specific points of entry or concentrated areas of infestation. We use precise application techniques to ensure the treatment reaches the termites directly.

We will explain the recommended treatment plan in detail, outlining the specific products and methods we will use. Our treatments are designed to be effective against termites while prioritizing the safety of your family and pets. We follow all industry regulations and best practices for safe and responsible pesticide application.

Signs You Might Need Termite Control in Englewood, CO

Recognizing the early warning signs of a termite infestation is crucial for minimizing damage and protecting your home. Termites are often elusive pests, and their activity can go unnoticed for extended periods. However, being aware of the common indicators can help you take prompt action. If you observe any of the following signs, it's highly recommended to contact professional termite control specialists like Aurora Termite Control for an inspection.

Discarded Termite Wings

One of the most common indicators of a termite swarm is the presence of discarded wings. Swarming termites, also known as alates, are reproductive termites that leave the colony to establish new ones. Once they find a suitable mate and location, they shed their wings. You might find these small, translucent wings near windowsills, doorways, or in spiderwebs. The presence of wings inside your home suggests that a termite colony is either within your walls or very close by and that swarming has recently occurred. Don't dismiss these wings; they are a clear signal that you have a termite problem that requires immediate attention.

Finding discarded wings can be alarming, but it's important not to panic. This indicates that a mature colony is nearby and attempting to reproduce. Taking swift action after noticing this sign can prevent further damage and potentially stop the establishment of new colonies within or around your home. Our team is experienced in identifying different types of termite wings and can confirm if they belong to destructive species.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundation

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, also known as shelter tubes, to travel from their colony in the soil to their food source (the wood in your home) while protecting themselves from open air and predators. These tubes are typically made of soil, wood particles, and termite excrement and are often found on exterior or interior foundation walls, pipes, or other structural elements. Mud tubes are a definitive sign of an active subterranean termite infestation.

Observing mud tubes is a strong indication that termites are actively foraging on your property and gaining access to your home. These tubes serve as highways for termites, allowing them to move safely between their underground nest and the wood they are consuming. Disturbing these tubes can sometimes reveal live termites inside. If you find mud tubes, do not delay in contacting a professional termite control service for a thorough inspection and treatment plan.

Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites feed on cellulose, which is found in wood. As they consume wood, they create tunnels and galleries within the structure, leaving behind a hollowed-out interior. If you tap on wooden surfaces, such as walls, floors, or trim, and they sound hollow, it could be an indication of termite damage. You might also notice crumbling wood or wood that appears to have been eaten from the inside out. Termite damage often starts beneath the surface, so it may not be immediately visible.

Discovering damaged or hollow-sounding wood is a significant concern, suggesting that termites have been actively feeding within your home's structure for some time. The extent of the damage may not be apparent from the exterior, and it's crucial to have a professional assess the severity of the infestation and the structural integrity of the affected areas. Early detection of wood damage can significantly reduce the cost and complexity of repairs needed.

Blistering or Bubbling Paint

Termites can damage not only the wood itself but also the surfaces covering it. As termites tunnel through wood beneath paint or wallpaper, they can cause the surface to bubble, blister, or peel. This is often mistaken for water damage, but it's essential to distinguish between the two. If you notice unusual bubbling or blistering on painted or wallpapered surfaces, investigate further, as it could be a sign of underlying termite activity.

While blistering paint can have various causes, including moisture issues, it should always be considered a potential indicator of termite activity, especially if accompanied by other signs. Termites can create moisture as they consume wood, and this moisture can lead to the blistering effect. Having a professional inspection can help determine if termites are the culprit behind the paint damage.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, which live within the wood they infest and do not require contact with the soil, often leave behind small, pellet-like droppings called frass. These droppings are typically found near small holes in the infested wood and can resemble sawdust or coffee grounds. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood being consumed. Finding piles of frass is a clear indication of a drywood termite infestation.

Frass is essentially termite fecal matter. The presence of these tiny pellets is a tell-tale sign that drywood termites are actively feeding and colonizing within your wooden structures. Unlike subterranean termites who live in the soil, drywood termites reside entirely within the wood, pushing their droppings out through small holes, giving you a visible clue to their presence. Identifying frass and its location helps pinpoint the area of infestation for targeted treatment.

Stiff Doors or Windows

As termites consume wood and create tunnels, they can cause structural damage that leads to doors and windows becoming stiff or difficult to open and close. This is because the wood around the frames may become warped or weakened due to termite activity. If you notice that your doors or windows are no longer operating smoothly, and you've ruled out other causes like swelling due to humidity, termites could be the underlying issue.

Doors and windows that stick or are difficult to operate can be a subtle but important sign of termite damage affecting the structural integrity of frames and surrounding wood. Termite tunneling weakens the wood, leading to misalignment and friction. Don't dismiss stiff doors or windows as just a minor inconvenience; it could be an indicator of ongoing termite damage that requires professional assessment.

Piles of Discarded Bodies

While finding individual dead termites isn't uncommon, discovering piles of dead termites, particularly after a swarm, is a strong indication of a recent or ongoing termite problem. You might find these bodies near windows, light fixtures, or other areas where swarming termites have gathered. While the swarm itself has occurred, the presence of dead bodies confirms that termites were present and active in your home or nearby.

Piles of dead termites are a visual reminder of a recent termite event. While the swarm may have passed, the colony from which they originated is still likely active and present. Cleaning up the dead bodies doesn't solve the underlying problem; it's a signal that a professional inspection is necessary to locate and eliminate the source colony. The presence of numerous dead termites suggests a significant number of reproductive termites were produced, indicating a well-established colony.

Early detection and intervention are key to minimizing the damage caused by termites and protecting the structural integrity of your home.
